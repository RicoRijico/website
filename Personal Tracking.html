What Is GPS Personal Tracking?

GPS personal tracking is the ability for any person to locate another person, object or thing with the use of a device that receives information from the GPS satellite network in space. All real-time personal GPS tracking systems include some sort of transmitter - that ultimately sends this data back to the end user. The most popular mode of transmitting the data back using GPRS through a cellular telephone company provider like AT&T or Sprint. For this reason, there are monthly subscription fees that the end user is responsible for just as with a cell phone. Some devices like the SPOT Personal Satellite Locater, transmit its information back up to a satellite, who receives the information and relays it and back down to earth.
